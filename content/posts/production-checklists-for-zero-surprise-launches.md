The most reliable launches are usually the least dramatic behind the scenes. That does not happen by chance. It comes from building a checklist that accounts for dependencies before event day.

Every production plan should cover ownership, timing, escalation paths, and fallback options. Vendor handoffs, power requirements, load-in windows, rehearsals, and sign-off points all need to be explicit.

On site, the checklist becomes a decision tool. It reduces ambiguity, keeps teams aligned, and makes quality control measurable instead of reactive.

If the plan only describes what should happen, it is incomplete. A useful checklist also defines what happens when things shift.
